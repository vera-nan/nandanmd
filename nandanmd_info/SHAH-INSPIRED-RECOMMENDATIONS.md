# Shah Plastic Surgery — What to Adopt for Nandan MD

Review of [Shah Plastic Surgery](https://www.shahplasticsurgery.com/) (Springfield, MO) and recommendations for nandanmd.com.

---

## What Makes Shah’s Site Strong

### 1. **Doctor quote in the hero**
- **Shah:** “My patients trust me as an expert surgeon, a compassionate doctor, and a member of their community.” — Bharat Shah MD. Placed prominently so the surgeon’s voice is front and center.
- **For Nandan:** Add a **pull quote from Dr. Nandan** (e.g. after the About section or in a dedicated block): “My patients trust me as an expert surgeon, a compassionate physician, and a partner in their care.” — Raghu Nandan, MD, FACS. Use a real quote when you have one.

### 2. **Category quick links on/near hero**
- **Shah:** Hero area includes big category buttons: Breast, Body, Men, Non-Surgical — each with “Learn More.” Visitors can jump straight to a category.
- **Nandan:** You have “One destination” with Surgical, Non-Surgical, Photo gallery. Optional: add **category pills** (Face, Eyelids, Breast & Body, Non-Surgical) near the hero CTA so the full scope is visible without scrolling.

### 3. **Spotlight procedure**
- **Shah:** “Spotlight Procedure: Facelift” — one procedure highlighted with a short description and “Learn More About Facelift Procedures.” Rotating or static spotlight focuses attention.
- **Nandan:** Add a **“Spotlight procedure”** section (e.g. Breast Lift or Eyelid Surgery) with 2–3 sentences and a link to that procedure page. You can rotate the spotlight over time.

### 4. **Named testimonials with procedure**
- **Shah:** Testimonials include first name + procedure type: “He was amazing!!! … I had a breast reduction…” — Amy, Breast Reduction. Builds trust and relevance.
- **Nandan:** On Patient Testimonials (and in the “What patients say” strip if you have consent), use **“First name, Procedure”** (e.g. “— Sarah, Breast Lift”) so readers see real outcomes by procedure.

### 5. **“Personal care and discretion”**
- **Shah:** Section “The Personal Care and Discretion You Deserve” — “committed to ensuring your comfort, privacy, and surgical results that inspire greater confidence.”
- **Nandan:** You already have “Discretion and personalized care” in Why Choose. Optional: add a short **standalone callout** with that phrase (e.g. a one-line block before CTA) to mirror Shah’s emphasis.

### 6. **Procedure categories with short blurbs**
- **Shah:** Plastic Surgery section: Breast, Body, Face, Men — each with one sentence (“Dr. Shah… performs an array of breast procedures…”) and “Learn More.”
- **Nandan:** “One destination” gives links; you could add **one sentence per category** (Face, Eyelids, Breast & Body) and a “Learn more” link to surgical-procedures.html or the specific procedure page.

### 7. **Patient advocacy / exceptional support**
- **Shah:** “Patient Advocacy — Exceptional support.” Copy about going above and beyond so patients have “true advocacy” and “access to the most advanced treatments.”
- **Nandan:** If it fits your practice, add a line in Why Choose or a small block: e.g. “We advocate for your comfort, privacy, and access to the best care at every step.”

### 8. **Gallery “Enter” CTA**
- **Shah:** Dedicated “Gallery” block with “Enter” as the CTA — creates a clear next step into before/after content.
- **Nandan:** You have a Photo Gallery link in nav and in “One destination.” Optional: add a **“View photo gallery”** card or button in the same area as “One destination” if you want to emphasize gallery more.

### 9. **Footer: star count**
- **Shah:** “4.5 Stars 86 Reviews” in the footer.
- **Nandan:** You have “Rated by patients on RealSelf & Doximity.” If you have a **Google or RealSelf star count**, add “X Stars from Y+ Reviews” in the footer for stronger social proof.

### 10. **Traveling patients**
- **Shah:** “Traveling Patients” — out-of-town coordination.
- **Nandan:** Add only if you actively serve **out-of-town or concierge** patients (e.g. Bay Area visitors from out of state).

### 11. **Video gallery**
- **Shah:** “Video Gallery” in the About dropdown.
- **Nandan:** If you have **patient or educational videos**, add a “Videos” link in the nav or on the About/Testimonials page.

### 12. **Nav structure**
- **Shah:** Mega-nav: About (with sub-pages), Face, Body, Breast, Men, Shah Aesthetics, Gallery, Shop. Very category-driven.
- **Nandan:** Your nav (Home, Surgical, Non Surgical, Photo Gallery, Testimonials, FAQs, Contact) is clear. Optional: add **dropdown or sub-links** for “Surgical” (Face, Eyelids, Breast, Body) if you want to match Shah’s category-first approach.
