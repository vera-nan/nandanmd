# How to Add or Update Photos on the Website (No coding)

**For the provider:** Use the site’s **admin panel** to add before/after photos and update site images. You only need a browser and your login link.

---

## Step 1: Open the admin panel

1. In your browser, go to: **https://YOUR-DOMAIN.com/admin**  
   (Use your real website address, e.g. `https://yourdomain.com/admin`.)
2. If asked, **log in** with the email and password you were given for the site admin.  
   (If you don’t have a login yet, ask the person who manages the site to send you an invite from Netlify.)

---

## Step 2: Add a before/after photo to a procedure

1. In the left sidebar, click **“Gallery (before & after photos)”**.
2. Click **“New Gallery”** (or “Add entry”).
3. Fill in:
   - **Procedure:** Choose the procedure (e.g. Breast Lift, Tummy Tuck, Eyelid Surgery).
   - **Image:** Click “Choose an image” and pick the file from your computer (e.g. a JPG or PNG).
   - **Caption (optional):** Short description (e.g. “45-year-old, mastopexy, 6 months post-op”). You can leave this blank.
4. Click **“Save”** (or “Publish”).
5. The new photo will appear on the website within a few minutes. You can add as many entries as you want; each one will show under that procedure’s “More patient results” section.

---

## Step 3: Update hero or banner images (optional)

1. In the left sidebar, click **“Site images (hero & banners)”**.
2. Click **“New Site images”** (or “Add entry”).
3. Choose **Image type:**  
   - **page-hero** = banner at the top of inner pages (e.g. Surgical, Contact).  
   - **home-hero** = main banner on the home page.  
   - **section-background** = background image for the “Our Approach” section on the home page.
4. **Image:** Upload the new photo.
5. **Notes:** Optional note for yourself (e.g. “New office photo”).
6. Click **“Save”**.

*Note: Right now the site uses these mainly for reference. If the live site doesn’t switch to the new image automatically, the person who manages the site may need to connect this to the pages (one-time setup).*

---

## Tips

- **File size:** Use images under about 2 MB so the site stays fast. JPG is best for photos.
- **Naming:** You don’t need to rename files; the admin will store them with a generated name.
- **Editing:** To change or remove an entry, open “Gallery” or “Site images,” click the entry, edit, and save.
- **Where photos show:** Gallery photos appear in the “More patient results” section on the procedure page that matches the procedure you chose (e.g. Breast Lift, Tummy Tuck).

---

## If something doesn’t work

- **Can’t open /admin:** Make sure you’re using **https://** and the exact address (e.g. `https://yourdomain.com/admin`). If it still doesn’t load, ask your site manager to check that the admin is enabled.
- **No login / forgot password:** Ask the person who set up the site to send a new invite or reset your password (they do this in the Netlify dashboard).
- **Photo doesn’t appear:** Wait 2–5 minutes after saving. If it still doesn’t show, try a different browser or ask your site manager to confirm the site redeployed.

You don’t need to edit any code or files—everything is done in the admin panel.
