Breast Reconstruction

Reconstructive

Restoring Form and Confidence

Breast reconstruction rebuilds the breast mound and, when desired, the nipple-areola complex after mastectomy or other surgery. Dr. Nandan offers implant-based and autologous (flap) reconstruction, and works closely with your oncology and care team to coordinate timing and approach.

Reconstruction can be performed at the time of mastectomy (immediate) or later (delayed). Your consultation will cover options, recovery, and what to expect at each stage. Many plans provide coverage for breast reconstruction — we can help with insurance questions.

Patient Results

Representative cases below. Individual results vary. View photo gallery

Nipple sparing mastectomy + Direct implants

This 38 year old female had bilateral nipple sparing mastectomy with immediate prepectoral direct to implant reconstruction using Natrelle 520cc full profile cohesive gel implants. She has a hidden scar below the breast with excellent symmetry and volume at 3-months postoperative.

Immediate DIEP flap reconstruction

This 45 year old female had a prior cosmetic breast augmentation with subsequent removal of implant and asymmetry. She had a bilateral mastectomy with immediate autologous reconstruction with DIEP flaps from the abdomen. She is shown on the right 12-months postoperative after scar revisions and fat grafting. Reconstruction has been done entirely using her own tissues without an implant.

Immediate implant reconstruction

This 60 year old female had bilateral mastectomy with immediate prepectoral direct to implant reconstruction using Natrelle 520cc full profile gel implants. She is seen on the right, 2 months postoperative with excellent volume and symmetry.

Staged prepectoral breast reconstruction

This 68 year old female had a staged reconstruction after bilateral mastectomy starting with tissue expanders above the muscle, followed by an exchange to Natrelle 560cc full profile gel implants. She is shown on the right 12-months postoperative with 3-D nipple tattooes.

Staged prepectoral breast reconstruction

This 49 year old female had a staged reconstruction after bilateral mastectomy starting with tissue expanders above the muscle, followed by an exchange to Natrelle 445cc full profile gel implants. She is shown on the right 12 months postoperative. Patient did not want nipple reconstruction.

Staged subpectoral breast reconstruction

This 45 year old female had a staged reconstruction after bilateral mastectomy starting with tissue expanders below the muscle, followed by an exchange to Natrelle 520cc full profile gel implants. She is shown on the right 12 months postoperative. Patient did not want nipple reconstruction.
