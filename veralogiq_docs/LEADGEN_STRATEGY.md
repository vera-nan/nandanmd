# Lead generation: UI/UX critique & strategy

## Critique (current state)

- **One-size-fits-all about page** — Teachers, parents, and students all land on the same long page. Teachers care about classroom permission; parents about tutoring; students just want to play. We don’t segment intent or offer a clear next step for each.
- **Missed CTA moments** — Math complete and Wild catch have CTAs. Trivia complete, Done for now, Kanto complete, and break/cooldown do not. Every “end of flow” is a chance to gently send someone to the right page.
- **No role-based entry** — Brand bar and footer only say “About us.” There’s no “For Teachers” or “For Families,” so educators and parents don’t see a path that’s clearly for them.
- **About page is dense** — Educator content is above the fold but tutoring is below; a parent may not scroll. Mixing everyone in one scroll reduces clarity and conversions.
- **Contact is generic** — One “Contact us on Instagram” for all. We don’t differentiate “DM for permission letter” (teachers) vs “DM for tutoring” (parents), so intent is lost.

## Strategy: four clear surfaces

| Page           | Purpose | Primary CTA | Secondary |
|----------------|---------|--------------|-----------|
| **About us**   | Hub: who we are, what we do, and three doors. | Send teachers → Teachers, parents → Parents, kids → Students. | Back to game. |
| **For Teachers** | Classroom permission, use our games, no forms. | “Play the game” + “Contact us for a permission letter” (Instagram). | Back to game. |
| **For Students** | “You’re playing our game; more coming.” Light, fun. | Play / return to game. | “Grown-ups: see For Families for tutoring.” (Indirect parent lead.) |
| **For Families** | Tutoring (math, coding, robotics), how it works, why us. | “Contact us on Instagram” for tutoring. | Back to game. |

## Don’t miss leads: CTA map

- **In-game**
  - **Brand bar:** About · For Teachers · For Families (so teachers/parents see their path without scrolling).
  - **Footer:** Same links so every scroll reaches a segment.
  - **Math complete:** Keep “Meet Veralogiq →”; link to About (hub) so they can choose Teachers or Families.
  - **Wild catch result:** Same → About.
  - **Trivia complete:** Add “More from Veralogiq →” to About (was missing).
  - **Done for now:** Add one line: “Made by Veralogiq — learning games & tutoring” → About (soft lead).
  - **Kanto complete:** Add “What’s next? More games & tutoring →” to About (high-intent moment).
  - **Break / cooldown:** No CTA (focus on break; avoid feeling pushy).

- **Off-game (About, Teachers, Students, Parents)**
  - Every page: small nav (About · For Teachers · For Students · For Families) so no dead end.
  - Teachers page: explicit “Contact us on Instagram” for permission letter.
  - Parents page: explicit “Contact us on Instagram” for tutoring.
  - Students page: “For Families” link so parents can discover tutoring.

## Principles

1. **Segment by role** — One hub (About), three audience pages. Clear labels so teachers and parents know where to go.
2. **One primary CTA per page** — Teachers: game + permission; Families: Instagram for tutoring; Students: play + soft nudge to Families.
3. **Capture every end-of-flow** — Trivia, Done, Kanto get a gentle CTA to the hub; Math and Wild already do.
4. **Keep it clean** — Short copy, one main button or link per block. No long forms; contact = Instagram only.
